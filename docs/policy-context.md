## Purpose

This document records the current external policy assumptions that shape the regularisation intake screener.

It is not legal advice and it is not a substitute for official instructions or operative legal text. It exists so policy-sensitive assumptions can be reviewed and updated without rewriting the design specification.

## Current context

As of April 2026, official Spanish government materials describe an extraordinary regularisation process for foreign nationals already in Spain. Public government statements have framed the policy around a formal, document-based process and have described criteria including presence in Spain before 31 December 2025, at least five months’ residence, and no criminal record.

Official materials also confirm the creation of the Registro Electrónico de Colaboradores de Extranjería under Order ISM/164/2026. This creates a formal framework for authorised organisations to assist with immigration-related electronic procedures, including extraordinary regularisation.

## Product assumptions

The product currently assumes that a useful first-touch screener should help establish:

- whether someone appears broadly aligned with the current policy direction
- whether their residence timeline appears relevant
- whether they report evidence that may support that timeline
- whether asylum or protection history may require specialist review
- whether missing identity documents or other flags make generic routing unsafe
- what kind of support or referral is needed next

These are triage assumptions only. They are not statements of legal entitlement or likely approval.

## Product constraints created by the policy context

The current policy context implies that:

- the service must be easy to update as policy details change
- support organisations may play an important intermediary role
